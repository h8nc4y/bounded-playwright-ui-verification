# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Participants are expected to keep discussion constructive, specific, and focused
on improving the project. Assume good intent, but prioritize clear evidence and
maintainable outcomes.

Examples of expected behavior:

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing experience levels and perspectives.
- Give feedback that is actionable and grounded in the repository.
- Accept maintainers' decisions about scope and project direction.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private information.
- Repeated off-topic promotion or spam.
- Pushing contributors to reveal secrets, credentials, customer data, or private
  logs.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and issues that violate this code
of conduct. Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently restrict
participation for behavior that harms the project or its contributors.

If you need to report a conduct concern, contact the maintainers through the
repository's GitHub moderation or reporting features.
